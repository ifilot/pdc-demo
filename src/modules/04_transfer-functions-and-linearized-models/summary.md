Transfer functions provide a compact input-output description of linear process
dynamics in the Laplace domain.

They are usually written in deviation variables, which makes it easier to work
around a nominal steady state and to connect the model to disturbances and
control actions.

Series combinations, poles, zeros, and first-order forms become much easier to
analyze in this representation. When a real process is nonlinear, linearization
around a steady operating point makes transfer-function analysis possible.
