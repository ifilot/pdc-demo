Laplace transforms turn linear differential equations into algebraic equations.
That is why they are so useful in process dynamics and control.

The most important operational rule is the transform of a derivative, because
it brings in the Laplace variable $s$ and includes the initial condition
automatically.

Standard transform pairs for constants, steps, ramps, exponentials, and
impulses make it possible to solve many engineering models efficiently. This
sets up the next major step in the course: transfer functions.
